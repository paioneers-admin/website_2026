# **Part One: The Story**

## **Bart’s Problem**

Bart Beentjes was a top-performing sales rep. Presidents Club at LinkedIn. Presidents Club at Salesforce. The kind of person who consistently hit 150% of quota while everyone around him scrambled to reach 80%.

But Bart had a problem that being good at selling couldn’t fix.

He was doing everything by hand. Researching prospects on LinkedIn before every call. Manually writing personalised emails from a blank screen. Updating his CRM at 7pm because there was no time during the day. Building his own spreadsheets to track which deals needed follow-up because the official systems were useless.

And here’s what made it worse: he was the exception. He did all this work because he was disciplined and talented enough to make it matter. His colleagues? They didn’t. They entered calls unprepared. They forgot to follow up. They logged garbage data into the CRM. And nobody noticed until the forecast came in wrong — again.

*"Forecasting was everyone’s nightmare. Because salespeople don’t log their stuff properly. They don’t update their opportunities. They don’t put the right value in there. The lower it goes in the organisation, the less control anyone has."*

**— Bart Beentjes, Co-founder, Paioneers**

Bart realised something that most sales leaders eventually figure out but few ever act on: the problem wasn’t the people. The problem was that there was no system. Every rep was running their own process. Every deal lived in someone’s head. Every forecast was a guess dressed up in a spreadsheet.

The companies that hired Bart weren’t small. They had CRMs. They had tools. They had sales managers and revenue targets and quarterly business reviews. But underneath all of that structure, the actual work — the daily reality of finding prospects, preparing for calls, following up, moving deals forward — was still being done the same way it was done in 1995\. ## **The Four Things That Break Before AI Can Help**

Before any automation works, four things need to be fixed. If you skip this step, you’re building on quicksand.

* **Unstructured information.** Your salespeople have hundreds of conversations. But the insights from those conversations live in their heads or in messy notes nobody reads. If the information isn’t structured, no system can use it.

* **Disconnected tools.** Your call recordings are in one place. Your pipeline data is in the CRM. Your email conversations are in another tool. They don’t talk to each other. The right hand doesn’t know what the left hand learned.

* **Incomplete data.** Half your deals are missing key fields. Contact information is outdated. Nobody knows when the last touchpoint happened. If you feed incomplete data into a smart system, you get confidently wrong answers.

* **No feedback loop.** When you lose a deal, what happens? Usually nothing. The learning never makes it back to the team that wrote the outreach or ran the discovery call. Every mistake gets repeated because the system has no memory.

This is exactly what the Bottleneck Workshop is designed to uncover. We sit down with your team, map the reality of how deals actually move through your process, and identify where the biggest leaks are. Then we build a roadmap to fix them — in priority order, starting with the quick wins that have the highest impact.
